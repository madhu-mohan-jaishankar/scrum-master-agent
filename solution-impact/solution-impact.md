# Solution Impact: Time Savings from the Scrum Master Agent

Research indicates that a Scrum Master dedicates between **11.5 and 15 hours per week** to core Scrum-related responsibilities for a single team. This includes roughly 1 hour on product backlog refinement, 0.75 hours on sprint planning, 1.5 hours on stand-ups, 0.5 hours on sprint reviews, 0.75 hours on retrospectives, and up to 5 hours on learning and training activities — totalling approximately half a standard working week (Rodeocat, 2020; KnowledgeTrain, n.d.). Beyond ceremonies, Scrum Masters spend around 1 hour per day preparing for meetings and up to 2 hours reviewing team reports, further illustrating the significant time investment required.

The proposed Scrum Master Agent directly targets these high-effort activities. For **sprint planning**, time is expected to reduce from 3–4 hours down to approximately 30 minutes, as the agent can auto-generate sprint structures based on backlog priorities. **Proposal writing** — currently 6–8 hours — is projected to drop to 45 minutes through automated document generation. **Sub-task breakdown**, currently a 45-minute manual process, is estimated to take just 10 minutes with agent assistance. **Onboarding and knowledge transfer**, typically a 2-hour activity, is projected to reduce to 15 minutes by leveraging the agent's contextual knowledge base.

These savings map directly to the following impact categories:

- **Reducing time on routine tasks**
- **Improving accuracy and consistency**
- **Reducing information search time**
- **Improving code quality**
- **Automating document generation**
- **Supporting onboarding and knowledge transfer**

Given that most of these activities recur on a **weekly or per-sprint basis**, the compound time savings are substantial — potentially recovering **10+ hours per sprint** per team, freeing leads to focus on higher-value strategic work.
